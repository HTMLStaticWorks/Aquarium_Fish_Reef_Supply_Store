The Pinnacle of Reef Keeping

Small Polyp Stony (SPS) corals are widely considered the holy grail of the marine aquarium hobby. Demanding high water flow, pristine water chemistry, and intense lighting, keeping SPS corals thriving is a rewarding challenge.

Our aquaculture facility specializes in cultivating rare strains of Acropora, Montipora, and Stylophora. By purchasing aquacultured frags, you are getting corals that are already adapted to aquarium life, making them hardier and more resilient than wild-caught colonies.

  • Over 50 unique color morphs

  • Shipped overnight in temperature-controlled boxes

Care Requirements

High PAR Lighting

Requires 250-400 PAR for optimal coloration and growth rates.

Strong Flow

Random, turbulent flow is necessary to keep detritus from settling.

Pristine Water

Nitrates < 5ppm and Phosphates < 0.03ppm are highly recommended.

Stability

Alkalinity, Calcium, and Magnesium levels must remain rock solid.

Tankmate Compatibility

Reef Safe Fish

Tangs, Wrasses, Anthias, and Clownfish are perfect companions. They won't nip at the polyps.

Angelfish

Dwarf and large angelfish may occasionally nip at SPS polyps. Add with caution.

Non-Reef Safe

Puffers, Triggerfish, and large wrasses may break or consume the coral skeleton.
