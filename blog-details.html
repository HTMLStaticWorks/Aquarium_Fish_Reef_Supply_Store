Reef Chemistry

Mastering Alkalinity & Calcium

August 12, 2026 | Dr. Elena Rossi

Understanding the delicate balance of the "Big Three" parameters is crucial for maintaining vibrant, fast-growing SPS corals. In this guide, we dive deep into the relationship between Alkalinity, Calcium, and Magnesium.

Corals build their calcium carbonate skeletons by extracting calcium and carbonates (alkalinity) from the water column. If either of these parameters drops too low, calcification stops entirely. More importantly, if alkalinity fluctuates rapidly, SPS corals like Acropora will experience rapid tissue necrosis (RTN).

Stability is more important than chasing a specific number. A stable alkalinity of 8.0 dKH is far better than fluctuating between 9.0 and 11.0 dKH within a week.

The Role of Magnesium

Magnesium is the unsung hero of reef chemistry. It prevents calcium and carbonates from precipitating out of the water. If your magnesium is below 1200ppm, you will find it nearly impossible to raise your calcium and alkalinity to proper levels.
